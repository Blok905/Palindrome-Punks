What is a Satoshi (Sat)?

A satoshi is the smallest transactable denomination of Bitcoin.

1 Bitcoin = 100,000,000 Satoshis

Therefore

1 Satoshi = 0.00000001 Bitcoin

What is a Palindrome Satoshi?

Based on the Ordinal Theory Handbook written by Casey Rodarmor, each Satoshi is assigned an integer relative to its position within the entire bitcoin supply. In this context, a Palindrome is a satoshi whose index number reads the same backward or forward.

They are highly sought after for their rarity and difficulty for bulk extraction. This is because it is highly unlikely to locate a cluster of consecutive palindromes within the circulating supply of bitcoin. Statistically speaking, approximately only one in every eighteen-million satoshis are Palindromes.

What is an Inscription?

Inscriptions inscribe sats with arbitrary data (JPEG/PNG, Audio, Video, GIF, TXT, JSON, HTML, Etc.), creating bitcoin-native digital artifacts, more commonly known as NFTs. Inscription content is stored entirely on-chain.
